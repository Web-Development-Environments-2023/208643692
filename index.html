    • This website is dedicated to the life of Moshe Katznelson,
    a former Palmach warrior who fought in Israel's Independance war.






    About Moshe

    Moshe was born on April 4th, 1930 in Tel-Aviv.
    He lived there until the age of 4, when the family moved to Haifa.
    Among his Tel-Aviv neighbors were David Remez (who
    would later be the Minister of Transport) and David Ben-Gurion.
    His father Iser (brother of Berl Katznelson),
    was a bus driver and later on a storekeeper,
    and his mother Sonya was a homemaker.
    Moshe studied in the "Hebrew Reali School" from 1st grade to 8th grade
    and then studied two more years in the maritime school near the Technion.
    In 1946, when he was only 16 years old,
    Moshe joined the Palmach while hiding his real age.
    After a period of service, Moshe returned to Haifa and started working on a ship.
    During his work Moshe traveled all over Egypt and also visited Yemen,
    Iran and Pakistan.
    On November 29th, 1947, the "partition plan" was approved in the UN.
    Israel's independance war began and Moshe joined the Palyam in Caesarea.
    He fought in the "Kastel battle" in Jerusalem and killed the leader of the arab forces.
    He would later on join the Navy and serve there until February, 1950.
    After his military service, Moshe and other former Navy members started a shipping
    company together with "ZIM".
    Life on the ship were full of thrills,
    and Moshe got to travel through Europe and North America.
    After a few years, Moshe decided to move to a new field
    and started working as a zoology teaching assistant,
    a class that he would later on teach himself.
    While serving in the reserves forces in 1954,
    his unit was stationed in Zemah near the Kineret,
    where he met Shosh who he would marry two years later.
    Moshe taught zoology for many years and worked in various zoology departments,
    including those of the Hebrew University in Jerusalem and Michigan University.
    After his return from Michigan, Moshe worked for "Mekorot" company,
    and the family moved to Kiryat Tiv'on.
    Moshe and Shosh had 6 kids, 18 grandkids, 14 great-grandkids and counting..
    Moshe passed away on November 5th, 2021 at the age of 91.
